BTC Riva 50 — Model Overview & Technical Guide

Added: 2026-05-11  |  Sources: btcbikes.com, 49ccscoot.com, ISRM community data  |  GY6 BTC 50cc Carburettor
📌 Primary sources: BTC Bikes — official brand site and 49ccScoot.com — GY6 community reference

The BTC Riva 50 is a popular 50 cc automatic scooter sold primarily in the UK and Western European markets by BTC Bikes (British Trading Company). It belongs to the broad family of Chinese-manufactured GY6-platform scooters — a category that also includes the La Souris Sourini, Santini Capri, Lexmoto Arizona, Pulse Aura, and dozens of other rebadged models sharing the same engine, CVT transmission, and chassis architecture.

For DIY repair purposes, the BTC Riva 50 is almost entirely interchangeable with any other GY6 139QMB-engine 50 cc scooter. Parts sourced for a generic GY6 50 cc will fit the Riva 50 in the vast majority of cases — this is one of the great practical advantages of the GY6 platform.

Carburettor

Most BTC Riva 50 scooters from 2015 onwards are fitted with a CVK18 constant velocity (CV) carburettor. Earlier models used a simpler PZ18 slide carburettor. The CVK18 has a vacuum-operated slide and is generally more tolerant of altitude variation; the PZ18 is simpler and easier to rebuild.

Standard jet sizes on the CVK18 for UK/Western Europe sea-level use:

  • Main jet: 75 (or 78 for some batches) — M5 × 0.8 thread
  • Pilot/slow jet: 38 or 40
  • Needle clip position: Middle groove (groove 3 of 5)
  • Air/fuel mixture screw (pre-set): 2.0 turns out from gently seated
💡 Jetting note: The Riva 50 is a restricted moped. Some owners fit a 35 mm exhaust restrictor insert and a small air-box restrictor at factory. These reduce power and can cause rich running at idle. If you are experiencing black smoke, remove and inspect the exhaust baffle/restrictor before re-jetting the carburettor.

Parts Compatibility Notes

The BTC Riva 50 uses the standard GY6 139QMB engine architecture. Parts from virtually all Chinese 50 cc GY6 scooters are interchangeable, including those from the Lexmoto range, Pulse Aura, Sinnis Apache, Keeway Matrix, and equivalent Belgian/French market models (Kymco Agility clones, etc.). When ordering parts, search for GY6 139QMB 50cc to ensure compatibility. Always compare the physical shape and dimensions of the part, as Chinese manufacturer part numbers are not standardised.

💡 Registration note (UK): The BTC Riva 50 is type-approved as an AM category moped in the UK and EU, limited to 45 km/h. It can be ridden from age 16 with a valid AM licence (or a CBT certificate in the UK). De-restricting the exhaust or air-box may affect the vehicle's type approval status.
