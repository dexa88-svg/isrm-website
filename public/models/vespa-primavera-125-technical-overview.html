Vespa Primavera 125 — Technical Overview

Added: 2026-05-08  |  Sources: manualslib.com, carlsalter.com, motorcyclemanuals.info  | 
Piaggio Vespa Fuel Injection iGet
📌 Based on: Piaggio official service station manual for the Vespa Primavera 125 (available via carlsalter.com), official owner's manuals at manuals.vespa.com, and community resources at Modern Vespa.

The Vespa Primavera 125 is Piaggio's modern entry-level Vespa, introduced in 2014 as a spiritual successor to the classic Primavera of the 1960s. It uses Piaggio's own iGet 125 engine (a 125cc single-cylinder 3-valve 4-stroke unit with electronic fuel injection and a catalytic converter) housed in a traditional monocoque steel body. Unlike GY6-platform Chinese scooters, the Primavera uses Piaggio's proprietary drivetrain and parts — not a shared-platform engine.

Oil Specification

Piaggio recommends SAE 5W-40 fully synthetic engine oil meeting API SN / JASO MA-2 for the iGet 125. Capacity is approximately 1.0 litre for a drain-and-refill without filter removal. If the oil filter is also changed, capacity rises to approximately 1.1 litre. Always fill to the upper mark on the dipstick, not the lower — overfilling causes foaming and seal damage.

Common Issues & Known Faults

  • Variator roller wear: The iGet 125 variator rollers (typically 15 g) wear flat over time, causing a top-speed reduction and sluggish low-speed acceleration. Inspect at every service; replace as a set.
  • Drive belt glazing: On models used mainly in urban stop-start conditions, the drive belt can glaze and slip before reaching its wear-limit width. If acceleration feels weak but the engine revs freely, inspect the belt surface for glazing and the clutch shoes for deposits.
  • Throttle body carbon build-up (Euro 4/5): Long service intervals on EFI models can cause carbon deposits on the throttle plate, causing rough idle and poor low-speed response. Clean every 24,000 km using a dedicated throttle body cleaner spray (do not use carburetor cleaner on plastic throttle bodies).
  • Front suspension creaking: The single-arm front suspension link pivot can develop a creak if the grease nipple is not regularly lubricated. Grease annually.
  • ABS warning light (Euro 4/5): Triggered most commonly by a dirty or damaged front wheel speed sensor. Clean the sensor and ring, check wiring connector.
