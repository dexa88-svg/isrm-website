The Piaggio ZIP is a lightweight urban scooter produced by Piaggio & C. S.p.A. in Pontedera, Italy. Available in 50cc and 100cc 4-stroke variants, it uses a Piaggio-designed single-cylinder engine with SOHC timing, forced-air cooling, and a KEIHIN CVK carburettor. Unlike GY6-platform Chinese scooters, every drivetrain component on the ZIP is Piaggio-proprietary and requires Piaggio original or equivalent quality parts. This page covers both the 50cc and 100cc variants. The 100cc carries engine prefix M252M and chassis prefix LEMM25200. The two variants differ in bore and stroke, carburettor throat diameter (Ø 18 mm vs Ø 20 mm), exhaust valve clearance, spark plug heat range, kerb weight, wheelbase, and front rim width — see dedicated sections below.

Engine Oil — Checking & Changing

The ZIP uses a dipstick on the right crankcase cover (no drain-plug sight glass). To check the level correctly:

  1. Park the scooter on level ground on the centre stand.
  2. Remove the dipstick, wipe it clean, reinsert it fully (screw in tightly), then remove it again to read the level.
  3. The oil level must be between the MIN and MAX marks. Do not overfill — excess oil causes foaming and oil consumption.
  4. To drain, unscrew the drain plug at the bottom of the crankcase. Torque to 25–28 Nm on refitting.
  5. Fill with approximately 850 cm³ of SELENIA HI SCOOTER 4Tech or equivalent SAE 5W-40 synthetic meeting JASO MA.

Rear Hub Oil — Checking & Changing

  1. Park on the centre stand on level ground.
  2. Unscrew the dipstick (cap A), wipe dry, reinsert fully, then remove and check level is between the 2nd and 3rd notches from the bottom (MIN to MAX).
  3. To change: remove the oil fill cap/dipstick and unscrew the drain cap (B). Drain fully.
  4. Refit drain cap and fill with approximately 80 cm³ of AGIP ROTRA 80W-90 (SAE 80W/90, API GL3).
  5. Hub oil must be changed at every 1,000 km or 4 months (whichever comes first) during the initial break-in period.

Air Filter — Cleaning Procedure

  1. Undo the 6 fixing screws and remove the air cleaner cover.
  2. Extract the foam sponge filter element.
  3. Wash with water and shampoo (household washing-up liquid is acceptable).
  4. Rinse thoroughly, then dry with a clean cloth — do not wring or compress the sponge.
  5. Soak the sponge in a 50/50 mixture of Selenia Air Filter Oil and unleaded petrol.
  6. Allow to drip dry on a clean cloth, then gently squeeze by hand to remove excess oil without distorting the sponge.
  7. Refit. Clean more frequently than the service schedule when riding on dusty roads.
⚠️ Never run without air filter: Operating the engine with a missing or damaged air filter allows unfiltered air and dust to enter the cylinder, causing accelerated bore and piston ring wear.

Common Issues & Known Faults

  • Excessive fuel consumption: Most commonly caused by a blocked air filter restricting airflow (engine runs rich), a stuck automatic cold-start (choke) device, loose main or pilot jets, or an incorrect float level in the KEIHIN carburettor. Clean the air filter and check the starter device mechanically before disassembling the carburettor.
  • Drive belt slipping or glazing: In urban stop-start use the drive belt can glaze before reaching its measured wear limit. If acceleration feels weak but the engine revs freely, inspect the belt surface for glazing and the clutch shoes for oil contamination.
  • CVT roller flat-spotting: The variator rollers develop flat spots over distance, causing a harsh, stepped sensation during acceleration and a top-speed reduction. Inspect at every 6,000 km service; replace as a complete set — never mix old and new rollers.
  • Rear wheel spinning at idle: Indicates idle speed set too high, or a faulty clutch with grease-contaminated friction masses. Verify idle is at 1,500 ± 150 rpm and inspect clutch masses for contamination.
  • Front brake spongy feel: Air in the hydraulic circuit. Bleed the front brake circuit using DOT 4 fluid; bleed valve torque is 8–12 Nm.
  • Oil weeping from crankcase joint: Crankcase half union screws (8–10 Nm) may need checking for torque and gasket condition if oil weeps from the centre joint.
