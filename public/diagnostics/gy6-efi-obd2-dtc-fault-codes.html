GY6 EFI — OBD-2 DTC Fault Codes & Diagnostic Procedures

EFI / Fuel Injection · Rongmao · Delphi · Motion · OBD-2 scanner required · Last updated: 2026-05-14
GY6 EFI OBD-2 / DTC Rongmao Delphi Motion EFI Fault Codes Diagnostics
Applies to: GY6 EFI scooters equipped with Rongmao, Delphi, or Motion injection systems — typically Euro 4 / Euro 5 models from approx. 2017 onwards (BTC Riva EFI, La Souris Sourini/City EFI, Santini Capri EFI, and equivalent Chinese 125/150cc 4-stroke scooters). These systems use standard OBD-2 P-codes readable via an OBD-2 scanner or apps such as Car Scanner or HUD ECU Hacker. If your scooter predates Euro 4 or uses a KEIHIN/clone ECU without OBD-2, see the CEL flash code guide instead.
ℹ️ This page vs the flash code page: Older GY6 EFI ECUs (KEIHIN PGM-FI clones) communicate faults as blink sequences — no scanner needed. Modern Rongmao, Delphi, and Motion ECUs speak standard OBD-2 DTCs (P0xxx codes) and require an OBD-2 reader. This page covers the OBD-2 / DTC system only.

What You Need

To read and clear fault codes on a Rongmao / Delphi / Motion EFI system you need an OBD-2 reader. The ECU communicates over the K-line (ISO 9141-2) or CAN bus depending on the variant. The following tools are confirmed to work:

  • Car Scanner (Android / iOS) — free app, works with a generic ELM327 Bluetooth or Wi-Fi adapter
  • HUD ECU Hacker (Windows PC) — purpose-built for Rongmao/Delphi GY6 ECUs; allows reading, clearing, and remapping
  • Any generic OBD-2 scanner that supports ISO 9141-2 / KWP2000
⚠️ Before reading codes: Check the MIL (malfunction indicator lamp) is working correctly by verifying it illuminates on key-on. Clear any history codes first, then repeat the fault condition and re-read. A code that appears only in history but not as a current fault may have been a one-off transient event.

Precautions (from Rongmao EFI Owner Manual)

  • Ensure battery positive and negative terminals are reliable and cannot be interchanged. When the engine is running, the battery cannot be disassembled.
  • If the fuel gauge alarm sounds, refuel as soon as possible — the fuel pump cannot operate in air or media other than fuel.
  • Do not arbitrarily disassemble vehicle parts, especially the fuel system (pipeline pressure approx. 250 kPa). Disassembly must be carried out by professional maintenance personnel to prevent fire risk.
  • Do not arbitrarily adjust the throttle valve door plate limit screw. Idle speed on EFI scooters is controlled automatically by the ECU via the IACV.
  • Vehicles may only use the same type of parts specified by the manufacturer. Turn off the ignition switch before disassembly or replacement of any electrical parts.
  • Never pierce the wiring harness to measure signals — use back-probing tools or proper breakout connectors.
  • Ensure the ECU temperature remains below 80 °C.

DTC Reference Table — All Codes

The following OBD-2 P-codes apply to GY6 EFI scooters using Rongmao, Delphi, and Motion injection systems. This table is aligned with the GY6 Injectie Centrum fault code reference and the Rongmao EFI Owner Manual. Detailed step-by-step diagnostic procedures for each code follow below.

DTC Code Component Fault Description Severity Typical Symptom
P0107 MAP sensor Manifold Absolute Pressure — low voltage or open circuit High Rough idle, stalling, poor performance
P0108 MAP sensor Manifold Absolute Pressure — high voltage High Rich running, black exhaust smoke, poor power
P0112 IAT sensor Inlet Air Temperature — low voltage or open circuit Low Slightly degraded fuel mapping, rarely noticeable
P0113 IAT sensor Inlet Air Temperature — high voltage Low Slightly degraded fuel mapping, rarely noticeable
P0117 ECT / CTS sensor Engine Coolant/Oil Temperature — low voltage or open circuit Medium Poor cold start enrichment, rough warm-up
P0118 ECT / CTS sensor Engine Coolant/Oil Temperature — high voltage or open circuit Medium Engine may over-enrich on warm engine
P0122 TPS sensor Throttle Position Sensor — low voltage or open circuit High Surging, hesitation, possible stalling on acceleration
P0123 TPS sensor Throttle Position Sensor — high voltage High Surging, incorrect fuelling across throttle range
P0131 Lambda sensor 1 (O₂S1) Upstream O₂ sensor — low voltage or open circuit Medium Lean running, closed-loop fuelling disabled
P0132 Lambda sensor 1 (O₂S1) Upstream O₂ sensor — high voltage Medium Rich running, closed-loop fuelling disabled
P0031 Lambda sensor 1 heater O₂S1 heater element — low voltage or open circuit Medium Slow O₂ sensor warm-up, open-loop fuelling longer at cold start
P0032 Lambda sensor 1 heater O₂S1 heater element — high voltage Medium Short circuit in heater circuit
P0137 Lambda sensor 2 (O₂S2) Downstream O₂ sensor — low voltage or open circuit Low MIL on; emissions monitoring affected
P0138 Lambda sensor 2 (O₂S2) Downstream O₂ sensor — high voltage Low MIL on; emissions monitoring affected
P0037 Lambda sensor 2 heater O₂S2 heater element — low voltage or open circuit Low MIL on; heater circuit fault
P0038 Lambda sensor 2 heater O₂S2 heater element — high voltage Low MIL on; short circuit in heater circuit
P0201 Fuel injector Injector cylinder 1 — circuit malfunction High Engine will not start or runs very poorly; misfire
P0202 Fuel injector Injector cylinder 2 — circuit malfunction High As P0201 (single-cylinder GY6 scooters only have cylinder 1)
P0230 Fuel pump relay (FPR) Fuel pump relay coil — low voltage or open circuit High No fuel pump prime on key-on; engine will not start
P0232 Fuel pump relay (FPR) Fuel pump relay coil — high voltage High Short circuit in relay coil circuit
P0351 Ignition coil Cylinder 1 ignition coil — circuit malfunction High No spark; engine will not start
P0352 Ignition coil Cylinder 2 ignition coil — circuit malfunction High As P0351 (not applicable on single-cylinder GY6)
P0444 PCV / Purge Control Valve Evaporative emission purge control valve — low voltage or open circuit Low MIL on; emissions system affected; drivability usually normal
P0445 PCV / Purge Control Valve Evaporative emission purge control valve — high voltage Low MIL on; short circuit in PCV circuit
P0500 VSS (Vehicle Speed Sensor) No speed signal detected Medium Speedometer inoperative; speed-dependent fuelling maps may be affected
P0505 IACV (Idle Air Control Valve) Idle Speed Control — system error Medium Idle too high, too low, or hunting; stalling at traffic lights
P0562 System voltage Battery / charging system voltage — too low Medium Multiple secondary sensor faults may appear; difficult starting
P0563 System voltage / regulator Battery / charging system voltage — too high High Overcharging; risk of ECU and battery damage — address immediately
P0650 MIL lamp Malfunction Indicator Lamp — circuit fault Low MIL lamp itself is defective or its circuit is open/shorted
P0850 Neutral switch Neutral position sensor — circuit fault Low Incorrect neutral detection; may affect idle speed in gear
P1693 Tachometer Tachometer signal — low voltage or open circuit Low Rev counter inoperative
P1695 Tachometer Tachometer signal — high voltage Low Rev counter erratic or pegged
Fault code legend: "Low voltage or open circuit" means the signal at the ECU pin is unexpectedly low — caused by a short to ground, broken wire, or disconnected sensor. "High voltage" means the signal is unexpectedly high — caused by a short to battery, open circuit in the return/ground path, or a failed sensor. Always check connectors and wiring before replacing sensors.

Symptom-Based Diagnostic Flows

If you cannot read a fault code, or want to verify the code is the root cause, use these symptom-driven checklists directly from the Rongmao EFI Owner Manual.

🚫 Engine Cannot Start
  1. Check battery voltage — should be 8–12 V
  2. Crank engine; check cranking voltage remains above 8 V
  3. Check starter motor operation
  4. In winter: check oil and gearbox oil viscosity
  5. Check engine rotation resistance is not excessive
⚡ Hard / Difficult to Start
  1. Check fuel pump pressure — approx. 250 kPa at idle
  2. Remove spark plug; check spark is normal
  3. Check engine temperature sensor connector and signal
  4. With slightly more throttle, check if engine starts — clean throttle body and bypass channel if yes
  5. Pull injector; crank engine — check injector spray pattern
  6. Check if spark plug is wet (flooding) — dry plug and combustion chamber
  7. Check cylinder compression pressure
〰️ Unstable Idle
  1. Check air filter and intake pipe for leaks or blockage
  2. Check throttle body for carbon deposits in bypass channel
  3. Check IACV operation
  4. Check fuel pressure — approx. 250 kPa
  5. Check injector for blockage
  6. Verify correct spark plug type and condition
  7. Check cylinder compression pressure
  8. Check engine temperature sensor signal
  9. Check TPS signal with throttle at idle
⬆️ High Idle
  1. Check throttle cable for sticking or incorrect adjustment
  2. Check idle pintle adjustment of throttle body
  3. Check intake pipe for air leaks
  4. Check IACV operation
  5. Remove engine temperature sensor; check if engine runs well — change sensor if yes
📉 Poor Acceleration
  1. Check air filter and intake pipe for blockage or leaks
  2. Check fuel pressure — approx. 250 kPa
  3. Remove spark plug — check if wet or dry
  4. Check T-MAP sensor, TPS, and all connectors
  5. Check injector for blockage
  6. Check spark plug type and electrode gap
  7. Check cylinder compression pressure
  8. Check exhaust pipe for blockage
💥 Backfire
  1. Remove spark plug; check spark is normal
  2. Check ignition timing is correct
  3. Check valve clearance for leakage
  4. Check injector for blockage or leaking
  5. Check oxygen sensor operation

Before Starting Fault Analysis — Checklist

From the Rongmao EFI Owner Manual — complete this checklist before beginning any fault code diagnosis:

  1. Confirm the MIL lamp works correctly (illuminates on key-on self-check).
  2. Clear history fault codes, then repeat the fault condition and re-read.
  3. When a new fault code appears, note the exact conditions (hot/cold, idle/acceleration, etc.).
  4. Visually inspect: check for fuel pipe leakage; check for blockage or damage to the intake pipe; check ground connections are secure; check all connectors are properly seated.
  5. If any visual defect is found, repair it first before proceeding with fault code diagnosis.
  6. If no cause is found after completing all steps: do not ignore maintenance items (cylinder pressure, mechanical timing, fuel quality). As a last resort, swap the ECU to test — if the fault disappears, the ECU was the root cause.

Clearing DTCs

On Rongmao / Delphi / Motion systems, codes must be cleared with an OBD-2 scanner — the battery-disconnect method is not guaranteed to clear stored codes on these ECUs (it will reset live data but not always non-volatile fault memory). Use Car Scanner or HUD ECU Hacker to send the "Clear fault codes" command after completing your repair.

⚠️ Always repair the root cause first. Clearing a code without fixing the underlying fault will cause it to return within minutes of running the engine.
