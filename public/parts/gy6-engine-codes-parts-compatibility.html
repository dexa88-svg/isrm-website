GY6 Engine Codes & Parts Compatibility Guide

The GY6 engine family is the most widely used small-displacement four-stroke engine platform in the world, powering hundreds of scooter models from manufacturers across China, Taiwan, and Europe. Understanding GY6 engine codes is essential for identifying the correct replacement parts, as the codes encode displacement, bore size, and cylinder head type directly into the engine designation.

How to Read a GY6 Engine Code

The engine code is stamped or cast on the lower side of the engine, near the CVT cover — just beside the oil drain bolt. It follows a consistent pattern across all variants.

Taking the code 157QMJ as an example:

Position Characters Meaning
1st digit 1 Number of cylinders (all GY6 engines are single-cylinder, so always "1")
2nd–3rd digits 57 Bore diameter in millimetres — 57 mm in this case (determines displacement)
4th character Q Cooling type: "Q" = forced air-cooled (not liquid-cooled)
5th character M Engine type: "M" = motorcycle/scooter/ATV application
6th character J Crankcase variant: "J" = horizontal case (scooter layout), "I" = inclined
⚠️ "P" Series Engines: Some engines have a prefix "1P" instead of just "1" — for example, 1P57QMJ. The "P" indicates a performance variant with a taller cylinder head and a different cam seating depth. The crankcase is the same dimensions, but the cylinder and cylinder head must be matched (both P or both non-P). Do not mix P and non-P top-end components without converting both together.

Crankcase Block Types: A-Block vs B-Block

Parts compatibility is primarily determined by the crankcase "block type", which controls cylinder stud spacing and overall engine dimensions.

Block Type Stud Spacing Engine Codes Notes
A-Block 54 mm 152QMI, 157QMJ, 1P57QMJ 125cc and 150cc GY6 — largest installed base, most aftermarket parts
B-Block 44 mm 139QMB, 1P39QMB 50cc GY6 — different cylinder, head, and variator sizes; NOT interchangeable with A-Block
⚠️ Critical Compatibility Rule: A-Block and B-Block parts are NOT interchangeable. A 157QMJ (150cc) cylinder cannot be fitted to a 139QMB (50cc) crankcase and vice versa. Always confirm your block type before ordering.

How to Locate Your Engine Code

  1. Place the scooter on its centre stand or lean it against a wall.
  2. Look at the lower-left side of the engine block, just above the oil drain bolt and beside the CVT cover.
  3. The code is stamped directly into the casting. It may be partially obscured by grime — clean with a cloth and solvent if needed.
  4. Photograph the code before ordering parts to avoid errors.

Big Bore Upgrade Compatibility

Big bore kits increase displacement by fitting a larger-bore cylinder and matching piston. Compatibility rules:

  • 150cc → 171cc (61 mm bore): Fits without crankcase modification on most A-Block engines. This is the recommended first step for performance upgrades.
  • 150cc → 180cc (63 mm bore): Requires crankcase boring/machining. Not a beginner job.
  • 50cc → 70cc (47 mm bore): Common upgrade for B-Block engines. Drop-in on most 139QMB crankcases.

When fitting a big bore kit, always re-jet the carburetor (or remap the EFI) to match the increased displacement. A larger bore burns more fuel at the same air/fuel ratio — running lean will damage the engine.
