GY6 Engine Oil Change Guide

The GY6 is a high-revving, air-cooled single-cylinder engine. Because it has no separate gearbox oil (the clutch and transmission share the engine oil), using the correct oil type and changing it regularly is critical. Neglected oil turns black and acidic within 2,000–3,000 km, causing accelerated clutch wear and premature piston and camshaft wear.

⚠️ Do NOT use car engine oil (API SN/SM) or oil with friction modifiers on GY6 engines. Car oils are formulated to reduce clutch friction — on a wet-clutch GY6, this causes clutch slip. Always use motorcycle-spec oil rated JASO MA or JASO MA2.

For European riding conditions (Belgium, Netherlands, UK): 10W-40 JASO MA2 is the best all-round choice for year-round use. Switch to 15W-40 in summer above 30°C if you notice increased oil consumption.

Tools & Materials Required

  • 17 mm socket or spanner (drain bolt, on most GY6 engines)
  • Drain tray or container (at least 1 litre capacity)
  • New oil (see specification above)
  • Clean rag
  • Replacement drain bolt washer (copper or aluminium crush washer, 12 mm) — recommended each change
  • Dipstick or level markings (built into the oil filler cap on most GY6 engines)

Step-by-Step Oil Change Procedure

  1. Warm the engine for 3–5 minutes. Warm oil flows more freely and carries more suspended contaminants with it when drained. Do not run to full temperature — the oil will be dangerously hot.
  2. Place the scooter on its centre stand on a level surface. Tilt the scooter slightly to the right to help all the oil drain toward the drain plug.
  3. Position the drain tray beneath the engine oil drain bolt. The drain bolt is located at the lowest point of the engine crankcase — usually a 17 mm bolt on the underside.
  4. Remove the oil filler cap (the dipstick/cap on top of the engine) first. This allows air to enter and improves drainage.
  5. Remove the drain bolt counter-clockwise. Hold the drain tray underneath — oil will flow immediately. Allow 3–5 minutes for full drainage.
  6. Inspect the drain bolt: Check for metal particles (silvery flakes = normal fine wear; large particles or chunks = investigate further). Clean the bolt threads with a rag.
  7. Replace the drain bolt crush washer if reusing; always use a new washer if available. Reinstall the drain bolt and tighten to 18–20 Nm. Do not overtighten — the aluminium crankcase threads strip at moderate torque.
  8. Refill with new oil through the filler cap opening. Add oil slowly — GY6 engines have a small capacity. Use a small funnel to avoid spills.
  9. Check the oil level using the dipstick or the marks on the filler cap. Insert the dipstick without threading it in (rest it on the threads), and read the level. Fill to the upper mark but not above it.
  10. Reinstall the filler cap and tighten firmly by hand.
  11. Start the engine and run for 2 minutes. Shut off and wait 2 minutes for oil to settle. Recheck the level and top up if needed.
  12. Check for leaks around the drain bolt and filler cap. Tighten if necessary.
💡 Tip: The GY6 dipstick is notorious for giving misleading readings on a slanted surface. Always check oil level with the scooter perfectly vertical and on the centre stand.

Oil Filter Screen (Clean Every 3,000 km)

Most GY6 engines do not have a replaceable spin-on oil filter. Instead, they have a strainer screen inside the crankcase near the drain bolt. Every third or fourth oil change, remove the screen and clean it with fresh oil or carburetor cleaner. A clogged strainer restricts oil flow and causes oil starvation in the engine top end.

On many GY6 50cc engines, the strainer screen is accessed by removing a small secondary plug beside the main drain bolt. Consult your engine's service manual for the exact location.
