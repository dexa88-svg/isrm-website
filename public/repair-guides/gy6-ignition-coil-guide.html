Ignition Coil (Bobine) — Diagnosis & Replacement

Added: 2026-05-07 | Sources: Buggy Depot Tech, YouTube — GY6 No Spark Diagnosis
repair-guides GY6 electrical ignition no-spark
📌 Applies to: GY6 50cc 139QMB, GY6 125/150cc 157QMJ. Use this guide when the engine cranks but does not start, or cuts out intermittently — especially when warm.

The ignition coil (French: bobine d'allumage) transforms the low-voltage pulse from the CDI unit into the 15,000–20,000 V needed to fire the spark plug. A failed coil results in no spark and a no-start condition. However, the coil is only one of four components that must all work together — always diagnose systematically before replacing parts.

How the GY6 Ignition System Works

The GY6 uses a CDI (Capacitor Discharge Ignition) system with four main components in the spark circuit:

ComponentFunctionFailure symptom
Stator / Pickup coilGenerates trigger signal as flywheel rotatesNo spark, weak spark
CDI unitStores charge, fires on trigger signalNo spark — most common failure
Ignition coil (bobine)Steps CDI pulse up to ~20,000 VNo spark, cuts out when hot
Spark plug + capProduces arc in combustion chamberNo spark, misfires
💡 On GY6 clones, the CDI unit fails far more often than the ignition coil. Always swap or test the CDI before buying a new coil. A replacement CDI costs €5–15; a coil costs €8–20.

Symptoms of a Faulty Ignition Coil

  • Engine cranks but will not start — no spark at the plug
  • Starts cold, cuts out when warm — classic thermal failure (coil breaks down under heat)
  • Intermittent misfires or sudden stalling while riding
  • Visible physical damage — cracked coil body, scorched or melted insulation, corroded HT lead connector
  • Weak orange spark instead of a sharp blue-white arc

Diagnostic Procedure — Step by Step

⚠️ High-voltage ignition circuits can deliver a painful shock. Do not hold bare HT wires while cranking. Keep fingers away from the spark plug cap end of the HT lead during testing.

Step 1 — Confirm No Spark

  1. Remove the spark plug from the engine.
  2. Reconnect the plug to the HT cap (spark plug cap).
  3. Lay the threaded body of the plug against bare engine metal (earth/ground).
  4. Crank the engine with the kickstart or electric start.
  5. Observe: bright blue arc = healthy spark. Weak orange glow or nothing = ignition fault.

Step 2 — Eliminate the Spark Plug

Substitute a known-good spark plug and repeat the spark test. A fouled or dead plug can mimic a coil failure.

Step 3 — Check the Kill Switch

  1. Locate the black wire running from the kill switch to the CDI/coil.
  2. Disconnect this wire completely.
  3. Try to start. If spark is now present, the kill switch is faulty — it is permanently earthing the circuit.

Step 4 — Check Earth Connections

A loose or corroded earth (ground) connection is an extremely common cause of no-spark on GY6 clones. Check:

  • Ignition coil mounting bolts — they must contact bare metal, not paint.
  • Battery negative terminal and its connection to the frame.
  • Engine earth strap from engine to frame.

Step 5 — Test the Coil with a Multimeter

Set your multimeter to resistance (Ω) mode:

Test pointHealthy readingFailed coil
Primary winding (between the two low-voltage terminals)0.3 – 0.5 ΩOpen (∞) or short (0 Ω)
Secondary winding (HT lead to earth terminal)3,000 – 7,000 ΩOpen (∞) or far outside range

If either reading is outside the normal range, the coil is defective and should be replaced.

Step 6 — Swap the CDI

If the coil tests OK, the CDI is the next most likely culprit. Swap it with a known-good unit (or a new €10 replacement). If spark is restored, the CDI was faulty.

Ignition Coil Replacement Procedure

  1. Disconnect the battery — negative terminal first.
  2. Locate the ignition coil. On GY6 engines it is mounted on the frame or engine bracket near the cylinder head — typically a black rectangular block with one HT lead going to the spark plug and two small wires (or a 2-pin connector) going to the CDI and kill switch.
  3. Remove the HT lead from the spark plug cap — unscrew counter-clockwise if threaded, or pull straight off if clip-type.
  4. Disconnect the low-voltage wiring connector.
  5. Unbolt the coil (2 × M6 bolts) from its mounting bracket and remove it.
  6. Fit the new coil — ensure the mounting bolts make clean metal-to-metal contact with the frame/engine for proper earthing. Tighten bolts firmly.
  7. Reconnect all wiring and the HT lead to the plug cap.
  8. Reconnect the battery (positive first, negative last).
  9. Test for spark as described in Step 1 above before reassembling any bodywork.
💡 Pro tip: Buy a spare CDI and ignition coil together (under €30 total for GY6 parts). Carry them when touring. Intermittent ignition failures that only appear when hot are impossible to diagnose by the roadside without a spare to swap in.

Applicable Models

This guide applies to all GY6 CDI ignition systems — 139QMB, 152QMI, 157QMJ engines used in BTC Riva, La Souris Sourini / City, Santini Capri, and all Chinese 50–150cc clones. Vespa Primavera and Sprint with electronic injection use a different ignition architecture (integrated ECU) — coil testing values and replacement procedures differ; consult the Piaggio workshop manual.
