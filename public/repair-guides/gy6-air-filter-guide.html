Air Filter — Clean or Replace

Added: 2026-05-07 | Sources: TaoTao Parts Direct, LookOver Maintenance Guide
repair-guides GY6 Vespa maintenance fuel-system
📌 Applies to: GY6 50/125/150cc (foam element airbox), Vespa Primavera / Sprint / ZIP (paper element). Clean every 3,000 km or every 6 months. Replace the element every 8,000–12,000 km or when damaged.

The air filter is one of the most overlooked maintenance items on small scooters, yet a clogged filter is one of the most common causes of poor performance, high fuel consumption, and a black sooty spark plug. The filter prevents dust, grit, and insects from entering the engine — a single unfiltered ride on a dusty road can cause more cylinder bore wear than 10,000 km of normal use.

🔴 Never run a GY6 scooter without an air filter fitted, even briefly for testing. Without filtration, abrasive particles enter the cylinder and cause rapid bore wear within minutes of running. If the filter is being washed and is still drying, wait — do not start the engine.

Symptoms of a Clogged Air Filter

  • Loss of power and acceleration — engine is starved of air, mixture is too rich
  • Increased fuel consumption — rich mixture burns more fuel for the same output
  • Black, sooty spark plug — carbon deposits from incomplete combustion
  • Difficulty starting, especially when warm
  • Black smoke from the exhaust
  • Rough idle that cannot be cured by carburetor adjustment alone

Tools & Materials Required

  • Phillips screwdriver or 8 mm socket (airbox cover screws)
  • Mild dish soap and warm water (foam filters)
  • Clean dry cloth or paper towels
  • Foam filter oil (available at motorcycle shops — do not substitute with engine oil)
  • Replacement filter element (foam or paper, model-specific)

Procedure — GY6 Foam Filter (Most Common)

  1. Locate the airbox. On GY6 scooters it is on the right side of the scooter under a body panel, connected to the carburetor inlet via a rubber intake boot. On most models two screws or a single wing nut secure the airbox cover.
  2. Remove the airbox cover and lift out the foam element. Note the orientation — some elements are directional.
  3. Inspect the element. If it is torn, deformed, or the foam has begun to crumble, replace it — cleaning a damaged filter is pointless. Also inspect the inside of the airbox housing for water, dirt, or insects and wipe it clean.
  4. Wash the foam element in a basin of warm water with a few drops of dish soap. Squeeze gently (do not wring or twist) and work the soap through the foam until the rinse water runs completely clear.
  5. Rinse thoroughly with clean water until all soap is gone. Soap residue left in the filter reduces airflow.
  6. Dry completely. Squeeze out excess water gently. Pat dry with a clean cloth and leave to air-dry for at least 30–60 minutes. The filter must be fully dry before re-oiling or reinstalling.
  7. Apply foam filter oil. Drizzle a small amount of filter oil onto the foam and work it in evenly with your fingers. The element should be lightly, evenly coated — not dripping wet. Excess oil gets drawn into the engine and fouls the spark plug.
  8. Reinstall the element in the correct orientation. Refit the airbox cover and tighten the screws or wing nut firmly — air leaks around the filter housing bypass the filter entirely.
  9. Check the intake boot (the rubber tube connecting the airbox to the carburetor) for cracks or loose clamps. A cracked boot allows unfiltered air to bypass the filter.
💡 No filter oil? In an emergency, a very thin smear of clean engine oil can be used on the foam, but proper foam filter oil has a tackier consistency that traps fine dust particles more effectively. Buy dedicated filter oil for the next service.

Procedure — Vespa / Paper Filter

  1. Access the airbox — on Vespa Primavera and Sprint models the airbox is under the left body panel. Remove the 2–3 screws securing the panel, then open the airbox cover.
  2. Remove the paper element and hold it up to a light source. If you cannot see light clearly through the filter paper, it must be replaced — paper filters cannot be washed back to full effectiveness.
  3. Tap the element gently against your palm to dislodge loose dust, if the element is only lightly soiled. Do not use compressed air — high pressure damages the paper fibres.
  4. Wipe out the airbox housing with a dry cloth.
  5. Fit the new or cleaned element and reclose the airbox. Ensure the cover seal is seated correctly — even a small gap allows unfiltered air in.

Paper filter — replaceNew element (do not wash)Every 6,000–8,000 km
⚠️ Aftermarket pod filters: Removing the stock airbox and fitting a cone/pod filter on a carbureted GY6 scooter requires carburetor re-jetting. The stock main jet is calibrated for the stock airbox restriction. A free-flowing pod filter leans the mixture and can cause overheating and piston damage if the carb is not rejetted to compensate.

Applicable Models

Foam filter procedure applies to all GY6-engined scooters: BTC Riva 50/125, La Souris Sourini / City, Santini Capri, and all Chinese 139QMB / 157QMJ clones. Paper filter procedure applies to Vespa Primavera 125/150, Vespa Sprint, and Vespa ZIP — consult your model year's service manual for the correct replacement part number.
