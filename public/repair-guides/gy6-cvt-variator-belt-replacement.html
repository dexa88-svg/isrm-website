GY6 CVT Variator & Drive Belt Replacement Guide

How the GY6 CVT Works

The Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) on GY6 scooters consists of three main assemblies: the variator (primary pulley on the crankshaft), the rear clutch bell/secondary pulley (on the transmission input shaft), and the drive belt that runs between them. As engine RPM rises, centrifugal rollers inside the variator push the pulley faces together, forcing the belt to ride higher — effectively changing the gear ratio automatically without any driver input.

⚠️ Safety first: Work on a cold engine only. The variator and clutch bell reach high temperatures during operation. Ensure the scooter is on its centre stand on a flat, stable surface.

Tools Required

  • Socket set (metric): 8 mm, 10 mm, 17 mm, 22 mm (or as required for your specific nuts)
  • Variator holding tool (universal GY6 type) — essential; do not attempt to use a screwdriver to jam the variator
  • Impact wrench or breaker bar (the variator nut is torqued very tight)
  • Torque wrench
  • Soft mallet or rubber hammer
  • Cleaning rags
  • Replacement belt (confirm dimensions — see Specifications below)
  • Replacement rollers if required

Step-by-Step Procedure

1
Remove the CVT Cover
  1. Place the scooter on its centre stand on level ground.
  2. Locate the CVT cover on the left side of the engine (the large plastic or alloy cover with ventilation slots).
  3. Remove all cover bolts — typically 6–8 bolts of 8 mm or 10 mm. Keep them together; they are often different lengths.
  4. Gently tap the cover with a soft mallet to break the gasket seal if it is stuck. Do not pry against the casting — it cracks easily.
  5. Remove the cover. Inspect the inside for belt dust, cracked rubber fragments, or oil contamination (indicates a crankshaft seal leak).
2
Remove the Drive Belt
  1. With the cover off, you will see the drive belt running between the two pulleys.
  2. Compress (push together) the faces of the rear clutch pulley by hand while simultaneously lifting the belt off the front variator pulley. This creates slack in the belt loop.
  3. Slide the belt off the rear clutch, then off the front variator. If the belt is stiff or cracked, it may snap during removal — wear eye protection.
  4. Inspect the belt: cracks along the edge, fraying, glazing, or visible rubber chunks missing all indicate the belt needs replacement.
3
Remove the Variator (for roller replacement)
  1. Use the variator holding tool to lock the front pulley against rotation.
  2. With an impact wrench or breaker bar, remove the variator nut (right-hand thread — turn counter-clockwise to loosen). This nut is typically torqued to 49 Nm (36 ft-lb).
  3. Slide off the outer face of the variator. Note the orientation — there is often an arrow or chamfer indicating the correct fitting direction.
  4. Remove the rollers. There are typically 6 rollers inside a guide cage. Inspect each one: flat spots, grooves worn into the roller surface, or irregular wear all indicate replacement is needed.
  5. Roller specifications by engine:
    • GY6 50cc (139QMB): 16×13 mm @ 7.5–8.5 g (OEM stock weight varies; aftermarket range 5–11.5g)
    • GY6 125cc (152QMI): 18×14 mm @ 12–13 g (OEM ~12.8g)
    • GY6 150cc (157QMJ): 18×14 mm @ 12–14 g
    Always use the correct diameter (16×13, 18×14, etc.) for your engine — mixing sizes causes CVT failure. Heavier rollers delay engagement (better top speed); lighter rollers engage earlier (better acceleration). Do not deviate more than 2 g from stock unless tuning for a specific purpose.
  6. Clean the variator ramp surfaces and roller guides with a rag. Do not use grease on the rollers — they must run dry. Grease attracts belt dust and causes premature wear.
  7. Install new rollers in the guide cage, reinstall the outer variator face, and torque the nut to 49 Nm.
4
Install the New Belt & Reassemble
  1. Route the new belt over the rear clutch pulley first, ensuring it sits centrally in the groove.
  2. While compressing the rear pulley faces together, loop the belt over the front variator. Release the rear pulley — the spring tension will pull the belt taut.
  3. Rotate the rear wheel by hand to seat the belt evenly and check that it tracks centrally on both pulleys without twisting.
  4. Clean the CVT cover interior and replace the gasket if torn or compressed flat.
  5. Refit the CVT cover. Tighten bolts in a cross pattern to approximately 10–12 Nm. Do not overtighten — the cover threads strip easily.
💡 Break-in tip: After fitting a new belt, avoid full-throttle acceleration for the first 100–200 km. New belts need time to seat and conform to the pulley surfaces. Premature hard acceleration can glaze or crack a new belt prematurely.
