Video Summary

This tutorial covers the essential carburettor adjustments that every owner of a carbureted modern Vespa (and by extension, Piaggio-platform scooters) should be able to perform themselves. The video is particularly relevant to Vespa models with the Dell'Orto PHBN or Dellorto SI carburettor, including the Vespa ET2 50, Vespa LX 50, Vespa S 50, Piaggio Zip 50, Piaggio Liberty 50, and similar 50–125cc 4-stroke Piaggio-group scooters using the Piaggio Leader or Zip engine family.

The techniques shown also apply broadly to any GY6-type or small 4-stroke scooter carburetor with the same idle speed screw and mixture screw configuration.

Key Points Covered

1. Prerequisites Before Adjusting

Carburetor adjustment should only be attempted when the following are confirmed to be correct:

  • Ignition timing is correct (spark plug gap in spec)
  • Air filter is clean and properly seated
  • No air leaks at the intake manifold or carb-to-airbox junction
  • Throttle cable has correct free play (1–2 mm at the twist grip)
  • Engine is at full operating temperature before any idle adjustment

2. Idle Speed Screw (Throttle Stop)

The idle speed screw contacts the throttle slide and sets the minimum throttle opening. It is usually located on the right or bottom of the carburettor body with a slotted flat head. Turning it clockwise raises idle RPM; anti-clockwise lowers it. Target idle speed for most 50cc 4T Vespa/Piaggio models is 1,800–2,000 RPM measured with a tachometer or judged by sound on a warm engine.

3. Air/Fuel Mixture Screw

The mixture (or pilot/fuel) screw regulates the air-fuel ratio at idle and light throttle openings. It is typically located on the engine-side of the carburettor float chamber, pointing down or at an angle. Many carburetors have this screw covered by a tamper-proof cap. Factory baseline setting is 1.5–2 turns out from lightly seated (clockwise to seat — do not overtighten).

  • Too lean (too far in): Engine surges, stalls when decelerating, runs rough at idle.
  • Too rich (too far out): Black sooty exhaust at idle, fuel smell, poor fuel economy.
  • To find the optimum: with the engine warm and idling, slowly turn the mixture screw out in ¼-turn increments. Stop at the position where idle RPM is highest. Then reduce idle speed back to target with the idle speed screw.

4. Throttle Cable Play

Before adjusting any mixture settings, ensure the throttle cable has the correct free play. With the handlebars turned to full lock in each direction, the idle RPM must not rise — if it does, there is insufficient cable slack and the carb slide is being lifted by the cable under steering input. Adjust the cable adjuster at the carburettor or handlebar grip.

💡 When to clean vs adjust: If your Vespa idles poorly and adjusting the mixture screw makes no perceptible difference, the idle jet (pilot jet) is likely blocked with fuel varnish. Remove and soak the jet in carburettor cleaner before attempting further adjustment. See the separate Carburetor Deep-Cleaning Guide for the full procedure.
