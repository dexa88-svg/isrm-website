This video is an advanced-level guide to the driven pulley (secondary clutch) and transmission clutch assembly on Vespa and Piaggio scooters. Unlike basic belt replacement videos, this covers full disassembly of the driven pulley unit — including contra spring removal and replacement — along with clutch shoe inspection, measurement, and replacement. The video emphasises the use of the correct special tools and explains the consequences of improper assembly.

⚠️ Advanced procedure: The contra spring in the driven pulley assembly is under significant preload. Compressing and releasing it without the correct spring compressor tool is dangerous. Do not attempt this procedure without the appropriate Piaggio special tool or an aftermarket equivalent.

Contra Spring Identification

Piaggio contra springs are usually colour-coded or have identifying marks. The contra spring rate directly affects how aggressively the driven pulley closes (which governs how quickly the belt shifts to the high-ratio position). A stiffer spring improves low-end pull; a softer spring allows quicker upshifts. Stock springs are appropriate for standard riding — only consider alternatives if you have modified the engine output.

Clutch Shoe Replacement Notes

Clutch shoes on Vespa and Piaggio scooters are measured as a set — replace all shoes together, never individually. When fitting new shoes, avoid touching the friction surface with bare hands (skin oils cause glazing). Bed in new clutch shoes gently over the first 50–100 km by avoiding aggressive acceleration from standstill until the shoes have seated.

Special Tools Required

  • Piaggio driven pulley nut socket (model-specific — check the official workshop manual for your model's socket size)
  • Contra spring compressor (Piaggio tool reference varies; universal scooter spring compressors are widely available)
  • Torque wrench (40–60 Nm range)
  • Clutch holding tool
  • Vernier caliper (for shoe thickness measurement)
