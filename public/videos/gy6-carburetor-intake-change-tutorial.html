Video Summary

This full tutorial demonstrates a complete carburetor and intake manifold replacement on a 150cc scooter fitted with a GY6 157QMJ engine — one of the most common engine platforms in Chinese-built scooters including the BTC Riva, Santini Capri, and many others. The procedure is filmed on a VIP-style bodywork scooter, but the engine access and component layout are directly applicable to any GY6-engined machine.

What the Video Covers

  • Removing the body panel and access cover to reach the carburetor
  • Disconnecting the fuel line, throttle cable, and choke linkage safely
  • Removing the intake manifold (inlet pipe) between the engine head and carburetor
  • Inspecting the old carburetor for wear, blockage, and gasket condition
  • Installing a new PD24J or equivalent carburetor on the replacement intake manifold
  • Reconnecting all cables and lines with correct routing
  • Initial idle adjustment and cold-start testing

Parts & Tools Referenced

  • Replacement carburetor: PD24J (standard for GY6 150cc / 157QMJ) — available from VMC Chinese Parts, Scooterworks USA
  • Intake manifold: GY6 150cc inlet manifold with carb-to-head gasket
  • JIS cross-head screwdrivers (#1, #2)
  • Needle-nose pliers (for fuel line clamps)
  • Flat-blade screwdriver (for carb clamps and jets)
  • New fuel line clamps (recommended if reusing old hoses)
