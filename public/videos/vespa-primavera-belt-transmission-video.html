When to Replace the Drive Belt

Piaggio recommends replacing the drive belt on the Primavera and Sprint every 20,000 km or 2 years, whichever comes first. Replace earlier if any of the following are present:

  • Visible cracking along the belt's inner face or across the top ribs
  • Belt width worn below the minimum (check current width against the specification in the service manual)
  • Glazed or shiny surface on the sides of the belt (indicates slippage history)
  • Any fraying at the belt edges
  • Vibration or jerking during acceleration — often the first symptom of a worn belt

Key Torque Specifications

  • Driven pulley centre nut: 50–55 Nm (Loctite threadlock recommended on reassembly)
  • Drive pulley (variator) nut: 45–50 Nm
  • Transmission cover bolts: 10 Nm

Tools Required

  • Piaggio variator holding tool (or universal equivalent)
  • Torque wrench (0–100 Nm range)
  • Socket set (metric)
  • Rubber mallet
  • Contra spring compressor (for driven pulley disassembly)
  • Soft-jawed vice (for contra spring work)

Genuine vs Aftermarket Belts

Piaggio OEM belts (part reference varies by market, check the parts catalogue for your VIN) are recommended for the Primavera and Sprint. Budget aftermarket belts vary widely in quality — if using an aftermarket part, choose brands such as Dayco, Gates, or Bando, which supply to OEM motorcycle manufacturers. Avoid no-brand belts for these Piaggio models as belt failure at speed can cause sudden loss of drive.
