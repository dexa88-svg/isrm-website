The One-Liner (Your Core Value Proposition)

A one-liner is a single, punchy sentence that captures the core idea of your product. It should be memorable enough that a user could repeat it to a friend. Think: Slack = "Where work happens", Figma = "The collaborative design tool". Your one-liner is NOT the full feature list—it's the essence.

Target User Persona

You're building for DIY scooter owners, not professional mechanics. But who exactly? Define them in detail: what they know, what they don't, what frustrates them, what motivates them. This guide every design decision later.
Example: "Alex owns a Xiaomi M365 e-scooter for commuting. They're tech-comfortable but not a mechanic. When something breaks, they search YouTube first."

3

The Three "Jobs to Be Done"

Your product likely solves 3 core problems. Think about the jobs your user tries to accomplish (not features, but outcomes). For ISRM, the roadmap suggests: find the right part, find the right procedure, diagnose the problem. But you should refine these.
🎯 Jobs to Be Done Framework: Instead of "features", think of the actual jobs users hire your product to do. E.g., not "search bar", but "find the right part quickly".
