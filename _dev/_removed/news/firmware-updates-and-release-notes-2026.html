What is Firmware & Why Update?

Firmware is the software that controls your electric scooter's motor, battery, braking system, and overall performance. Manufacturers release firmware updates to improve performance, fix bugs, enhance safety, and address security vulnerabilities. Keeping firmware current is essential for optimal functionality and safety.

Common Reasons for Firmware Updates

  • Performance improvements: Refined motor control algorithms, better acceleration response, improved efficiency
  • Bug fixes: Resolving display glitches, sensor errors, or app connectivity issues
  • Safety enhancements: Improved braking stability, speed limiting, and fault detection
  • New features: Support for new accessories, riding modes, or app functions
  • Security patches: Addressing vulnerabilities in the controller's security systems
  • Battery optimization: Better charge management and longevity algorithms

How to Update Firmware Safely

Before You Update

  • ☑️ Fully charge your scooter battery (at least 50%, ideally 100%)
  • ☑️ Connect your scooter to the official app (ensure Bluetooth connection is strong)
  • ☑️ Back up any custom settings or profiles
  • ☑️ Ensure your phone has adequate battery life
  • ☑️ Read the release notes to understand what is changing
  • ☑️ Find a stable, quiet location for the update (avoid bumpy surfaces)

Standard Update Process (App-Based)

  1. Open your scooter's companion app
  2. Navigate to "Firmware," "Updates," or "Device Settings"
  3. The app will detect available updates
  4. Tap "Update" or "Install" and confirm
  5. The app will download the firmware file (usually 5–50 MB)
  6. Once downloaded, the scooter will begin the update process
  7. Do not disconnect or power off the scooter during this time
  8. The update typically takes 5–15 minutes
  9. Your scooter will restart automatically when complete
  10. Verify the new version in Device Info

If Your App Doesn't Show Updates

  • Check the manufacturer's website for latest firmware versions
  • Try unpairing and re-pairing your scooter to the app
  • Ensure your app is updated to the latest version
  • Some regions may receive updates on different schedules

Best Practices

  • Update proactively: Don't wait for critical errors. Regular updates keep your scooter optimized.
  • Read release notes: Understand what's changing before updating, especially if you've customized settings.
  • Test after update: Take a short test ride in a safe area after firmware updates.
  • Avoid updating while traveling: Update at home where you can troubleshoot if needed.
  • Track versions: Keep a record of which firmware version you're running for troubleshooting reference.
