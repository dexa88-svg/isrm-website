Complete Guide to Electric Scooter Firmware Updates

Why Firmware Updates Matter

Firmware is the low-level software that runs on your scooter's controller and battery management system (BMS). Manufacturers release firmware updates to fix bugs, improve performance, address safety issues, enhance battery longevity, and add new features. Keeping your scooter's firmware current ensures optimal operation and warranty compliance.

What's In a Typical Firmware Update?

  • Performance optimizations: Smoother acceleration, improved braking response
  • Compatibility improvements: Better app connectivity, sensor calibration updates
  • Bug fixes: Resolving error codes, fixing display glitches
  • Battery management enhancements: Better charging algorithms, improved cell balancing
  • Safety patches: Addressing known vulnerabilities or hazardous conditions

How to Update Your Scooter Firmware

Official App Updates (Recommended for Most Users)

Most modern scooters (Ninebot, Xiaomi, Segway, Apollo, etc.) support over-the-air (OTA) updates via the manufacturer's mobile app.

Step-by-step:

  1. Download the official app for your scooter brand (Ninebot app, Xiaomi Home, Segway app, etc.).
  2. Pair your scooter via Bluetooth if not already done.
  3. Navigate to the Device Settings or Firmware menu in the app.
  4. Tap Check for Updates or Firmware Update.
  5. If an update is available, the app will show the version number and release notes. Tap Install or Download & Install.
  6. Keep the scooter powered on and Bluetooth connected during the entire process (usually 5–15 minutes).
  7. Once complete, the app will confirm success. Power off and back on the scooter to activate the new firmware.
⚠️ Critical: Do not disconnect Bluetooth, turn off the scooter, or lose battery power during a firmware update. Interruption can corrupt the firmware and render the scooter inoperable.

Updating Xiaomi Electric Scooters

Xiaomi scooters (M365, M365 Pro, 3, 4, etc.) update through the Xiaomi Home or Mi Home app (depending on region and app version).

  1. Open the Xiaomi Home app and tap your scooter's tile.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner.
  3. Select Firmware update or Check for firmware.
  4. Follow the on-screen prompts. The update typically takes 10–20 minutes and requires the scooter to be connected via Bluetooth.

Updating Ninebot Scooters

Ninebot scooters (ES, E-series, Max, Air, T15, etc.) update via the Ninebot App.

  1. Open the Ninebot app and ensure your scooter is paired.
  2. Navigate to the scooter's profile page.
  3. Scroll down to Firmware Update or Device Firmware.
  4. Tap Check Update. If available, the app shows the new version and improvements.
  5. Tap Update Now. Keep the scooter powered on and close to your phone during the process.

Manual Firmware Flashing (Advanced Users)

Some enthusiasts and experienced users manually flash custom or stock firmware onto their scooters using utilities like the ScooterHacking Utility or manufacturer-provided tools. This requires technical knowledge and voids most warranties.

Why manual flashing?

  • Accessing firmware for older or discontinued models
  • Installing custom firmware with unlocked features (e.g., CFW for Xiaomi scooters)
  • Recovering a scooter with corrupted firmware
  • Downgrading to a stable version if a new release has bugs

General process (varies by scooter):

  1. Download the firmware file from ScooterHacking GitHub or the manufacturer's support page.
  2. Connect the scooter to your computer via USB or BLE adapter.
  3. Use the flashing utility (ST-Link Utility, ScooterHacking Utility, or similar) to load the firmware.
  4. Follow the tool's prompts carefully. Interruption or wrong firmware can brick the scooter.
  5. After flashing, disconnect and reboot the scooter.
⚠️ Warning: Manual firmware flashing is risky. Only attempt this if you're comfortable with technical tools and accept the risk of making the scooter inoperable. Back up your original firmware file before flashing anything.

Firmware Update Best Practices

  • Charge fully before updating: Ensure the battery is at 80–100% to avoid power loss during the process.
  • Use a stable Bluetooth connection: Update in a location with strong phone signal and minimal interference.
  • Read release notes: Check what the update addresses. If it fixes a bug you've experienced, prioritize the update.
  • Update regularly: Check for updates quarterly or after major incidents (e.g., firmware corruption reports in forums).
  • Avoid updating during bad weather: Wait for dry conditions to prevent interference with Bluetooth connectivity.

Troubleshooting Firmware Updates

Update Stuck or Failed

  • Power off the scooter and disconnect Bluetooth.
  • Wait 2 minutes, then power on and reconnect to the app.
  • Try the update again. If it fails repeatedly, check for app updates or try from a different phone/device.

Scooter Won't Start After Update

  • Leave the scooter powered off for 1–2 minutes, then press the power button again.
  • The BMS may be recalibrating after the update. Wait up to 5 minutes before concluding there's a problem.
  • If it remains unresponsive, contact the manufacturer's support team with the firmware version you attempted to install.

Performance Issues After Update

  • Power cycle the scooter and reconnect to the app.
  • If the issue persists, check community forums (Reddit, ScooterHacking) to see if others report the same problem.
  • Some updates require you to re-calibrate throttle or brakes in the app settings. Consult your manual.
