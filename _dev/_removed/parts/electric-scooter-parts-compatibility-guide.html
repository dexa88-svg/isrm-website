Understanding Parts Compatibility

Unlike bicycles or many motorcycles, electric scooter parts are not universally compatible. Each manufacturer—Xiaomi, Ninebot, Kaabo, Dualtron, Apollo, etc.—designs proprietary components. A battery from a Xiaomi M365 will not fit a Ninebot Max, and a motor from a Kaabo Mantis will not work in a Dualtron Eagle.

However, some consumable and generic parts can be swapped:

  • Tubes and tires: Can often be used across scooters with the same wheel diameter (e.g., 8-inch inner tubes for 8-inch wheels from different brands)
  • Deck lights and accessories: Generic LED lights, bells, mirrors with universal mounts
  • Phone holders and grips: Aftermarket clamps that fit standard handlebars
  • Brake pads: Sometimes compatible if the brake mechanism is similar (mechanical friction brakes)

Critical Parts (Brand-Specific)

These parts are locked to specific brands and models:

Battery Packs

Each scooter model has a custom-designed battery with a unique form factor, connector type, and battery management system (BMS) integration. You cannot swap batteries between brands or even between different models from the same brand (e.g., Xiaomi M365 battery ≠ Xiaomi 3 battery).

Finding the right battery:

  • Check your scooter's model number on the bottom of the deck.
  • Search for "[Your Model] replacement battery" on the manufacturer's website or authorized retailers.
  • Confirm voltage (36V, 48V, 52V, etc.) and watt-hour capacity (Wh) match your original.

Motors and Motor Wheels

Motors are tightly integrated with the wheel hub or suspension frame. Swapping motors between brands is not practical; even different models from the same brand may have incompatible power connectors or control protocols.

When replacing a motor:

  • Order the motor from the manufacturer or authorized parts distributor for your model.
  • Confirm wattage and voltage compatibility.
  • Installation may require professional technician skills (wheel truing, electrical safety).

Controllers and Electronic Control Units (ECUs)

The controller is the computer that manages acceleration, braking, and motor performance. It's programmed for the specific scooter model and cannot be used on a different model or brand.

Battery Management Systems (BMS)

The BMS protects the battery and integrates with the scooter's display and app. Replacing a BMS requires a programmer/technician unless you buy a pre-assembled battery pack.

Semi-Compatible Parts (With Caveats)

Tires and Inner Tubes

This is where you can often find compatibility across brands, if the wheel diameter matches.

Common electric scooter wheel sizes:
  • 6 inches: Razor E100, E200 (older models)
  • 8 inches: Xiaomi M365, Ninebot ES, some entry-level models
  • 10 inches: Ninebot Max, Kaabo Mantis, many mid-range scooters
  • 11 inches: Some Dualtron models, premium scooters
  • 12+ inches: Heavy-duty and off-road scooters

Finding compatible tires:

  1. Check your current tire sidewall for the size (e.g., "8×2.0" means 8-inch diameter, 2.0-inch width).
  2. Search for tubes or tires of the same size. Brands like CST, Kenda, and Vee Rubber make tubes for electric scooters.
  3. Choose pneumatic (air-filled) tubes if your scooter has pneumatic tires, or solid-foam tubes if your scooter uses solid/semi-solid tires.
  4. Cross-check the valve type: most e-scooters use Schrader valves (like car tires), though some older models use Presta valves.

Brake Pads

Brake compatibility depends on the brake type:

Mechanical friction brakes (drum or disc brakes with pads): Can sometimes use generic replacement pads if dimensions match. Brands like EBC and Sintered make compatible pads for some scooters. Always confirm fit before purchasing.

Electronic brakes (regenerative or magnetic brakes): Typically not user-replaceable and brand-specific. Service centers handle replacements.

Grips and Handlebars

Many aftermarket grips fit standard handlebar diameters (usually 16–22 mm). Check that your handlebar diameter matches the grip's clamp size. Generic rubber grips and ergonomic replacements are widely available.

Lights and Accessories

LED front/rear lights with universal mounting brackets are compatible with most scooters. Look for lights that offer adjustable clamp widths and state compatibility with e-scooters.

How to Identify Your Scooter Model

  1. Look at the bottom of the scooter deck for a label or sticker with the model number (e.g., "M365 Pro," "Ninebot Max G30").
  2. Check the app associated with your scooter (Xiaomi Home, Ninebot App) for the model listed in device settings.
  3. If the sticker is worn, take a photo of the scooter from all angles and search online for lookalikes, or contact the brand's support team with your photos.

Verifying Part Compatibility Before Purchase

  • Confirm model match: Search for "[Your Exact Model Number] [part type]" (e.g., "Xiaomi M365 Pro battery").
  • Check specifications: Voltage, watt-hours (for batteries), wattage (for motors), and physical dimensions must match.
  • Ask the seller: Contact the retailer's support team with your model number and ask if the part fits. Reputable sellers will confirm compatibility.
  • Review community forums: Check r/ElectricScooters or brand-specific subreddits to see if others have used the same part successfully.
  • Request a return policy: Purchase parts from retailers that offer returns if the part doesn't fit.

DIY Part Upgrades (Advanced)

Some enthusiasts perform upgrades like installing a higher-capacity battery or upgrading to a more powerful motor. This is risky and requires electrical knowledge, as it can:

  • Void the warranty
  • Damage the scooter if the controller can't handle the new power
  • Create safety hazards (brakes may not stop a heavier or faster scooter)

Only undertake upgrades if you understand the electrical implications and can handle the scooter's weight and performance safely.
