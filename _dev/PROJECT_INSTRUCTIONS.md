# Interactive Scooter Manuals for Repair (ISMR) - Project Instructions

## Project Overview

The ISMR project is about building an interactive website with comprehensive guides (videos, pictures, text, etc.) that help users choose the right parts and perform repair procedures correctly.

This platform should function similarly to ETKA for VAG, where repair manuals and technical data are available, but with enhanced interactivity featuring 3D models and other interactive elements.

## Content Strategy

- Content should be automatically or regularly updated from reputable sources such as:
  - Professional technical websites
  - Manufacturer sources
  - Established forums and technical communities
  - Video hosting platforms (YouTube, etc.)
  - Community contributions (Wiki-style model)

- The platform will start as a website and can evolve into a more interactive chat-like model with mobile applications in the future

## MVP Scope

For the Minimum Viable Product, focus on gasoline/petrol scooters only:

**Models:**
- GY6-based scooters (BTC Riva, La Souris Sourini/City, Santini Capri, etc.)
- Piaggio brand (Vespa, Primavera, Zip)

**Fuel System Coverage:**
- Carburetor-equipped models
- Injector/fuel injection-equipped models

## Content Guidelines & Quality Standards

### Prohibited Content

The following types of content are **strictly prohibited** from all project materials:

1. **Sexually Explicit Content**
   - No adult or sexually suggestive material of any kind
   - All content must be family-friendly and suitable for all audiences

2. **Hateful & Discriminatory Content**
   - No racist, sexist, homophobic, transphobic, or otherwise discriminatory content
   - No hate speech or content targeting protected groups
   - No slurs or derogatory language toward any group

3. **Dangerous & Illegal Content**
   - No content promoting illegal activities
   - No instructions that could cause harm or injury
   - Repair procedures must prioritize safety

4. **Harmful & Unethical Content**
   - Avoid widely known bad practices or dangerous methods
   - Do not include content from sources known for misinformation
   - Exclude controversial conspiracy theories or pseudoscience

### Language & Tone Standards

- **Formal, Professional Language Only:** All content must use business/formal language appropriate for technical documentation
- **Technical Accuracy:** Maintain accuracy in all repair instructions, specifications, and technical data
- **Clarity & Precision:** Instructions must be clear, unambiguous, and technically sound
- **No Informal Slang:** Avoid colloquialisms, internet slang, or casual speech

### Source Validation

- **Trustworthy Sources Only:** Link to and source content from:
  - Manufacturer websites and official documentation
  - Established technical forums with moderation
  - Professional repair guides and technical databases
  - Peer-reviewed or professionally-curated content
  
- **Risky Links & Websites:** Exclude or flag:
  - Unknown or unverified sources
  - Websites with security warnings
  - Sources with poor reputation or credibility issues
  - Potentially malicious links or phishing sites
  - Sites promoting counterfeit parts or scams

### Community Contribution Standards

For user-submitted or Wiki-style contributions:
- Review all submissions against these guidelines before publishing
- Require sources for technical claims
- Flag and remove violating content promptly
- Maintain moderation to ensure quality standards
